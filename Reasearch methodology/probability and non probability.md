# Probability and Non-Probability Sampling Methods

Sampling means selecting a small group (sample) from a large population for research.

---

# 1. Probability Sampling

### Definition

In probability sampling, **every member of the population has a known and equal chance of being selected**.

### Features

* Random selection
* Less bias
* More accurate results
* Suitable for scientific research

### Types with Examples

### A) Simple Random Sampling

Every member has an equal chance of selection.

**Example:**
A college has 100 students. Select 10 students using a lottery method.

---

### B) Systematic Sampling

Select every kth item after a random start.

**Example:**
From a list of 100 employees, choose every 10th employee.

---

### C) Stratified Sampling

Population is divided into groups (strata), and samples are selected from each group.

**Example:**
A school has 60 boys and 40 girls. Select students proportionally from both groups.

---

### D) Cluster Sampling

Population is divided into clusters, and entire clusters are selected randomly.

**Example:**
A city has 20 schools. Randomly select 5 schools and survey all students in those schools.

---

# 2. Non-Probability Sampling

### Definition

In non-probability sampling, **every member does not have an equal chance of being selected**.

### Features

* Non-random selection
* Faster and cheaper
* More bias
* Less accurate than probability sampling

### Types with Examples

### A) Convenience Sampling

Select people who are easily available.

**Example:**
Surveying students standing near the college gate.

---

### B) Judgment (Purposive) Sampling

Researcher selects participants based on knowledge or judgment.

**Example:**
Interviewing experienced doctors about healthcare policies.

---

### C) Quota Sampling

Population is divided into categories, and a fixed number is selected from each category.

**Example:**
Survey 50 males and 50 females for a product review.

---

### D) Snowball Sampling

Existing participants help identify new participants.

**Example:**
Research on drug addicts where one participant refers another.

---

# Difference Between Probability and Non-Probability Sampling

| Probability Sampling                | Non-Probability Sampling         |
| ----------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| Random selection                    | Non-random selection             |
| Equal chance of selection           | Unequal chance of selection      |
| Less bias                           | More bias                        |
| More accurate                       | Less accurate                    |
| Suitable for large research studies | Suitable for exploratory studies |

# Conclusion

**Probability Sampling** uses random selection and gives every population member an equal chance of being chosen, whereas **Non-Probability Sampling** uses non-random selection where chances of selection are unknown.

### 2-Mark Exam Definition

> **Probability Sampling:** A sampling method in which every member of the population has an equal and known chance of being selected.

> **Non-Probability Sampling:** A sampling method in which the selection of samples is based on convenience or judgment, and every member does not have an equal chance of being selected. ✅
