# Difference Between Quantitative Research and Qualitative Research

| Quantitative Research                                        | Qualitative Research                                            |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Deals with **numerical data**.                               | Deals with **non-numerical data**.                              |
| Focuses on **measurement and statistics**.                   | Focuses on **opinions, experiences, and behavior**.             |
| Uses **structured methods** like surveys and questionnaires. | Uses **unstructured methods** like interviews and observations. |
| Results are presented in **numbers, tables, and graphs**.    | Results are presented in **words, descriptions, and themes**.   |
| Usually involves a **large sample size**.                    | Usually involves a **small sample size**.                       |
| Objective and fact-based.                                    | Subjective and interpretation-based.                            |

## Example

**Quantitative Research:**

* Surveying 500 students to find their average study hours per day.

**Qualitative Research:**

* Interviewing 20 students to understand their study habits and learning experiences.

---

## Short Exam Answer (4–5 Marks)

**Quantitative Research** focuses on numerical data and statistical analysis, while **Qualitative Research** focuses on understanding opinions, experiences, and behaviors through non-numerical data. Quantitative research uses surveys and experiments, whereas qualitative research uses interviews and observations.
